Welcome to Landhub

Land Hub is a first step in exploring opportunities for ‘blended’ or ‘stackable’ finance so that farmers and landowners can receive income from more than one source for the public goods offered.

The Cornwall Catchment Partnership was established in 2014 and is working to secure a sustainable, clean water environment throughout Cornwall for people and wildlife. Collaboration sits at the heart of the Partnership’s approach as a key mechanism to better integrate decision making and ‘on the ground’ delivery in relation to water and the environment.

There has been a major shift in Government policy in recent years as a result of Brexit; a move away from the Common Agricultural Policy and the arrival of the 25 Year Environment Plan with the aspiration to leave our environment in a better state than we found it. We are all getting to grips with thinking in terms of natural capital, carbon credits, nature recovery, biodiversity net-gain, a new Environmental Land Management Scheme and public money for public goods.

Amongst all of this change there is one thing that is clear – we will only be successful in achieving any of our aspirations if we truly work in partnership, bringing together the strategic need to deliver multiple benefits and communicating the natural capital opportunities to farmers and landowners so that they can become an integrated part of the farm business, thus supporting local delivery.

Land Hub provides access to locally targeted data and information to allow different efforts, resources and expertise to be joined up in a complimentary approach.

The Catchment Partnership is committed to supporting the farming community in taking advantage of the new environmental land management system (E.L.M.S), where farmers are rewarded with public money for public goods whilst also recognising that this needs to become an integrated part of a profitable and productive farm business.

Land Hub is a first step in exploring opportunities for ‘blended’ or ‘stackable’ finance so that farmers and landowners can receive income from more than one source for the public goods offered. It will also provide a means through which strategic organisations can communicate funding opportunities directly to the farming community for interventions such as those which will help to reduce surface water runoff and flood risk.
